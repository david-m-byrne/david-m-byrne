I focus on the effect of information technology on economic growth and the organization of economic activity. If you're working in this area, I'd be interested to hear about it.

It's easy to point to what IT can do (say, draw a picture of a horse driving a car) and jump to the conclusion it will change the economy. Finding and measuring that change is far harder. Much of my work has been directed toward improving measurement of the production and use of IT. Existing data does not support serious analysis of the life cycle of IT innovation, its effect on productivity growth, and the changing structure of the tech sector supply chain. Targeted improvements to measurement of production, investment, consumption, and depreciation have been effective, but insufficient. I'm focused on building a database covering stocks and flows for all stages of the global electronics industrial sector with cross-country, time-series, and cross-product consistency, ensured by a common approach to measurement.

Papers

Broad Scope
  • The Digital Economy and Productivity: Overview of the digital economy, its role in aggregate productivity, and measurement challenges that interfere with analysis.
  • Is the IT Revolution Over?: Assessment of the prospects---as of 2013---for a revival of rapid productivity growth boosted by IT. (with Dan Sichel and Steve Oliner)
  • Does the United States have a Productivity Slowdown or a Measurement Problem?. Assessment of the contribution of measurement problems---those quantifiable as of 2016---to the reported productivity slowdown since the boom that began in the 1990s. Correcting these problems seems unlikely to significantly reduce the slowdown. (with John Fernald and Marshall Reinsdorf)
  • IT Services and their Prices: What do they tell us about Productivity and Technology?. Overview of prices of computing & communications equipment and software, best practices for measurement, and implications for productivity and technology. (with Carol Corrado)
  • IT Asset Prices: Marshaling Evidence into New MeasuresEstimates, sources, and methods for computing & communications equipment and software for 1963-2015.
  • Acounting for Innovations in Consumer Digital Services: IT Still Matters. Accounting for consumer access to digital services---both purchased and generated by household IT equipment---shows that IT consumption has accelerated. (with Carol Corrado)
Narrow Scope
  • The Rise of Cloud Computing: Minding your P's, Q's, and K's. Sketches the nature and size of cloud computing and offers guidance on measuring the nominal value and the price of cloud computing investment
  • Prices for Communications Equipment: Rewriting the Record. Sources, methods, and estimates for the full range of communications equipment types. (with Carol Corrado) Recent trends in communications equipment prices is a short treatment of the same issues.
  • Quality-adjusted Price Measurement: A new Approach with Evidence from Semiconductors. An approach to measuring contract chip manufacturing using explicit modeling of the substitution between suppliers. (with Briak Kovak and Ryan Michaels)
  • Prices for Data Storage Equipment and the State of IT Innovation. Data storage equipment---often overlooked in discussions of IT---exhibits rapid price declines when measured well.
  • Prices and Depreciation in the Market for Tablet Computers. Joint estimation of prices and depreciation. (with Wendy Dunn and Eugenio Pinto)
  • The Increasing Deflationary Influence of Consumer Digital Access Services. Prices for internet, mobile phone, cable TV, and streaming services are 2 percent of U.S. household consumption and their prices are falling much quicker than is widely understood. (with Carol Corrado)
  • The Scope of U.S. "Factoryless Manufacturing". Firms that outsource fabrication but retain control of design are poorly captured in official statistics and are widespread across industries. (with Kim Bayard and Dominic Smith)
  • How Fast are Chip Prices Falling? We show that when performance on benchmark tasks is used to measure chip quality, microprocessor prices continued to fall rapidly through 2013. (with Dan Sichel and Steve Oliner)
  • Prices for High-Tech Products, Mismeasurement, and the Pace of Innovation. The wedge between innovation in the IT sector and the rest of the economy is wide, and substantially wider than statistics show. (with Dan Sichel and Steve Oliner)
  • Getting Smart about Phones: New Price INdexes and the Allocation of Spending Between Devices and Services Plans in Personal Consumption Expenditures. Demonstrates rapid declines in prices for smartphones result from hedonic quality adjustment and that PCE is missing a noteworthy share of smartphone consumption. (With Ana Aizcorbe and Dan Sichel)
  • Domestic Electronics Manufacturing: Medical, Military, and Aerospace Equipment and What We Don't Know about High-Tech Productivity The footprint of the electronics sector in the U.S. manufacturing sector is almost entirely special-purpose equipment, which have received little attention.
